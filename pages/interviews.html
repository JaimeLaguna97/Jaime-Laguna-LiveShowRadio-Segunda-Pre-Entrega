Indira Paganotto

I usually spend almost the whole day in the studio, but at the beginning of the pandemic I didn't understand anything and I couldn't make music, it was a shock, I imagine for everyone. With David, my boy, I decided to start with streaming and use different backgrounds, since I saw that the ones people did from home were very similar. We reused the materials that we had here, in the field, since at that time it was impossible to leave. We made one with trees as if it were a forest, then another with paintings... Luckily, with this I was having a lot of fun.

Baume: electronic music after black metal│Interview

A few years ago, the musician Gaetan Juif was dedicated to atmospheric black metal, composing and playing in bands that have quite a few followers: Cepheide and Basilique. But his, on a musical level, covered a much broader spectrum of sounds that, in the long run, ended up coming into direct contact with various styles of electronic music. With this idea, his Baume project was born, which in times of confinement released an EP, Un Calme entre les Tempêtes. It has earned the label of experimental, although it is simply a somewhat lazy way of cataloging sounds that encompass very diverse electronic influences.

Fixon (ANAØH Records): «From art we learned to resist by loving»

Fixon, born in the eighties in Mexico, is one of the pillars of techno and electronic music both in his country and in Latin America and Europe. Warm and affable, agile with words possibly thanks to his interest in literature and all kinds of art, with a fine and elegant sense of humor, in mid-2021 I was lucky enough to share an extensive talk with the founder of ANAØH Records and Vector Functions along with Dig-it.
